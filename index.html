Salesforce Introduction

As customers have become the pivot around which the growth of any company revolves, it has become extremely important for companies to deploy appropriate customer relationship management (CRM) software solutions. However, customer requirements are diverse, and most companies do not find a suitable CRM solution that best fits their needs. In this situation, Salesforce has emerged as a market leader. With flexibility and reliability as its primary features, Salesforce is most likely to continue dominating the marketplace in the coming years.

If you are a developer, you know the pain of following numerous code components in order to create an application. Well, Salesforce comes to your rescue. With Salesforce, you can create applications in a few days or weeks and make them visible to as many clients as you want with the provided customizations.

Why Should We Learn Salesforce?

Salesforce started as a Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) company providing a CRM platform on the cloud. Along with SaaS, Salesforce provides a platform where its clients can create their own applications. This is called Platform-as-a-Service (PaaS). Although most of Salesforce’s revenue comes through PaaS, SaaS is also used by many Fortune 500 companies.

Salesforce CRM

Salesforce CRM helps companies manage relationships with their customers and integrate with other important systems. Salesforce CRM is a SaaS tool that aids companies in creating personalized solutions for their marketing, services, sales, and e-commerce problems. All Salesforce CRM data is cloud-based, so it can be accessed remotely from anywhere.

What is SFDC?

SFDC stands for Salesforce.com, an official website of Salesforce. It was launched in 1999 by Marc Benioff. You can visit the website and register on it. You can also start your free trial for 30 days. This will help you get hands-on experience with SFDC.

Why SFDC?

  • SFDC altogether reduces the development time and cost and delivers the applications in a much shorter time.
  • SFDC allows the buying and selling of applications. SFDC’s AppExchange feature has proven to be a good marketplace for users to sell their custom-made applications.
  • SFDC’s availability on the cloud makes it accessible from anywhere in the world.

History of Salesforce

Founded in 1999 by Marc Benioff, Salesforce began with a humble San Francisco origin. Its IPO in 2004 raised an impressive $110 million. The company’s significant milestone came in 2014 with the introduction of the Customer Success Platform, integrating sales, service, marketing, analytics, and more. Collaborations with Facebook and Apple further enhanced its offerings. Salesforce’s global presence expanded with services provided in 16 languages, serving a vast customer base exceeding 150,000.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Salesforce, and why is it used?

Salesforce.com is a SaaS company that leverages cloud computing to design CRM software for organizations. This CRM software can be used by salespeople to track sales, support people to track queries, and the workforce to collaborate.

Introduction to Salesforce for Beginners

In the previous section of this Salesforce tutorial, you got an Introduction to Salesforce. So, What is Salesforce? This section will explain the actual Salesforce meaning in detail.

Salesforce is a cloud-based software company that provides its customers with a platform to develop their own applications without following the tough steps that they used to follow in the legacy system. The software or application once created can be uploaded onto the cloud allowing the end-users to view them.

Salesforce in Action

Talking about HR systems, every company across the globe has an HR team. Each HR team would require an HR application to store employee records. Almost all specifications for such an application would be common for all companies. So, as a developer, it would be very easy to create a Salesforce application for such specifications, post it onto the cloud, and provide it as a service to multiple clients at the same time.

Salesforce Cloud Services

Salesforce offers a variety of "Clouds" designed for specific business needs. Here are the major clouds you should know:

Sales Cloud

The most popular and trusted cloud service. It is very useful for companies that require a lot of follow-ups with respect to leads and customers. Sales Cloud makes the work of a sales representative very easy as it gives a consolidated view of all customers.

Marketing Cloud

Used for marketing purposes. You can send customized mass mailers to thousands of potential customers at just a single click. You can also target content on social media using advertisements to boost sales numbers.

Service Cloud

Using Salesforce, support agents can address customer problems with the right tools. Building self-service communities gives your customers the ability to solve their issues on their own terms.

Analytics Cloud

Analytics Cloud can be integrated with many other clouds. You can get data from all clouds and give an analysis of the problems that arise time-to-time.

Community Cloud

Salesforce Community Cloud lets you directly connect with customers, increase partner success, and drive employee productivity. Multiple communities can be created for specific needs and customized to represent any brand.

Commerce Cloud

A cloud-based e-commerce platform that can manage products while providing a wide range of facilities. It delivers a comprehensive digital experience to customers and enhances the shopping experience.

App Cloud

Imagine having the power to create apps! Salesforce App Cloud is a new kind of PaaS that helps you do the same. It is an ecosystem for building, discovering, and running all your apps.

Collaboration Cloud (Quip)

This cloud changes the way a team works together, from combining data, documents, spreadsheets, etc. It is used to connect to the content and communicate with the team.

What is a Salesforce Administrator?

A Salesforce Administrator is a business leader who knows how to use the features and functionality of the platform to solve business problems.

They are responsible for managing users, data, security, and building automation within the Salesforce environment.

Salesforce Lightning Components

Salesforce Lightning is a modern user interface framework developed by Salesforce. It is designed to help developers create enterprise applications faster.

It provides out-of-the-box components that make building apps easier than with the classic interface.

Salesforce Marketing Cloud

Marketing Cloud is a digital marketing platform that allows businesses to create and manage marketing relationships and campaigns with customers.

It supports email, social media, and mobile marketing, as well as data analysis and audience management.

Salesforce CRM

Salesforce CRM (Customer Relationship Management) is a technology for managing all your company’s relationships and interactions with customers and potential customers.

It helps improve profitability by streamlining processes and ensuring customer satisfaction.

Salesforce Architecture

The architecture of Salesforce is designed to be scalable, secure, and multi-tenant. It consists of different layers:

  • Multi-tenant Architecture: Multiple customers share the same infrastructure.
  • Metadata-driven: The behavior of the application is driven by metadata.
  • API Access: Everything in Salesforce is accessible via APIs.

Creating a Salesforce Application

An app in Salesforce is a logical container for a set of tabs, objects, and other items that work together to solve a specific business problem.

You can create apps using the App Manager in Setup.

Salesforce AppExchange

AppExchange is Salesforce's marketplace for business applications. It's like an "App Store" for your CRM.

You can find thousands of pre-built apps and components to extend Salesforce functionality.

Salesforce Objects Tutorial

Objects are essentially database tables in Salesforce. They store data.

Standard Objects: Pre-built objects like Account, Contact, Lead.

Custom Objects: Objects you create to store information specific to your organization.

Creating Custom Fields

Custom fields allow you to capture specific data points on objects.

Navigate to: Object Manager > [Select Object] > Fields & Relationships > New.

Reports and Dashboards

Reports: Lists of data based on specific criteria.

Dashboards: Visual representations of report data, showing key metrics at a glance.

Wrapper Class in Salesforce

A wrapper class is a custom class defined by a developer to display different objects on a Visualforce page or Lightning component.

It acts as a container to hold multiple data types in a single unit.

Governor Limits in Salesforce

Because Salesforce is a multi-tenant environment, it enforces limits to ensure no single organization monopolizes shared resources.

Examples include limits on SOQL query rows (50,000), CPU time, and heap size.

Process Builder

Process Builder is a point-and-click tool that lets you easily automate if/then business processes.

It is more powerful than Workflow Rules and allows for multiple actions in a single process.

Workbench in Salesforce

Workbench is a powerful, web-based suite of tools designed for administrators and developers to interact with Salesforce organizations.

It is commonly used to run SOQL queries, REST API calls, and migrate data.

Batch Apex

Batch Apex is used to process large numbers of records that would exceed normal processing limits.

It breaks the processing into manageable chunks to avoid hitting Governor Limits.

Workflow in Salesforce

Workflow rules automate standard internal procedures and processes to save time across your organization.

They consist of: 1) Criteria (If), 2) Actions (Then).

Triggers in Salesforce

Apex triggers enable you to perform custom actions before or after events to records in Salesforce, such as insertions, updates, or deletions.

Syntax: trigger TriggerName on ObjectName (trigger_events) { ... }
